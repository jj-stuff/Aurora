Aurora

The aurora dances across the night sky like a celestial ballet, its vibrant colors swirling and weaving a mesmerizing tapestry of light.

It is as if a painter's brush has been set free upon the canvas of the atmosphere, creating an ethereal masterpiece that is both fleeting and eternal.

History

Aristotle described aurora as "northern dawn" in the 4th century BCE, and thought it was caused by sunlight reflecting off clouds. It wasn't until the 18th century that scientists discovered that aurora is caused by charged particles from the sun interacting with the Earth's magnetic field.
